## 1. Executive Summary
This research project conducts a comprehensive digital business model audit of the German retail banking sector, specifically focusing on the **Sparkassen-Finanzgruppe**. By integrating live data from the **Deutsche Bundesbank**, **ECB**, and **Eurostat**, the project identifies the "Burning Platform" of traditional branch-heavy banking and proposes a redesigned digital-first business model. The implementation utilizes a full-stack data pipeline (Python ETL), version-controlled analytics (Power BI), and automated CI/CD (GitHub Actions), mirroring the "Integrative Project" methodology of the DigiBIM program.

## 3. Technical Implementation (The "Behind the Scenes")

### A. Data Acquisition (Python ETL)
Using Python (`pandas`, `requests`), I developed a programmatic ETL pipeline to fetch real-world financial indicators:
- **Interest Rate Benchmarks**: Sourced from the Deutsche Bundesbank to model revenue sensitivity.
- **Digital Adoption Metrics**: Sourced from the ECB to quantify market shift.
- **Branch Density Data**: Sourced from Eurostat for EU-wide benchmarking.

### B. Automation (CI/CD)
The project uses **GitHub Actions** to run the ETL scripts weekly. This ensures the research is not a static snapshot, but a "live" market audit that responds to real-world economic changes.
